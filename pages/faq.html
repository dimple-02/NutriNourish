What is NutriNourish?

NutriNourish is a comprehensive nutrition platform that helps you track your meals, calculate BMI, access healthy recipes, and understand your dietary patterns. Our tools are designed to make nutrition management simple and sustainable.

Do I need to create an account?

You can use most features without an account. However, creating a free account allows you to save your nutrition data, track progress over time, and access personalized recommendations. Sign up here to get started.

Is NutriNourish free to use?

Yes! All core features including nutrition tracking, BMI calculator, and recipe access are completely free. We believe everyone should have access to quality nutrition tools.

What is a Nutrition Tracker?

A nutrition tracker is a digital tool that helps you record what you eat and drink throughout the day. It logs food items, portion sizes, calories, macronutrients (protein, carbs, fats), and helps you understand your eating patterns better.

How does nutrition tracking help me?

Tracking your nutrition provides several benefits:

  • Awareness: See exactly what you're eating each day
  • Identify patterns: Discover habits that help or hurt your goals
  • Meet nutritional needs: Ensure you're getting enough protein, vitamins, etc.
  • Weight management: Adjust calorie intake to lose, gain, or maintain weight
  • Better health: Make informed decisions based on real data
  • Accountability: Stay consistent with your nutrition goals

How accurate does my tracking need to be?

Aim for consistency over perfection. Even rough estimates provide valuable insights. For best results, weigh portions when possible and be honest about what you eat. Tracking 80% accurately is better than not tracking at all.

Can I track macronutrients (protein, carbs, fats)?

Yes! Our advanced nutrition tracker allows you to log all macronutrients along with calories. You'll see running totals for the day so you know exactly where you stand with your macro goals.

What is BMI and how does it work?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a screening tool that estimates body fat based on your height and weight. It's calculated by dividing weight by height squared.

Formula: BMI = weight (kg) / height (m)�

What do BMI categories mean?

BMI ranges are interpreted as follows:

  • Below 18.5: Underweight
  • 18.5 - 24.9: Normal weight
  • 25.0 - 29.9: Overweight
  • 30.0 and above: Obese

Note: BMI is a screening tool, not a diagnostic measure. Consult a healthcare provider for personalized advice.

Is BMI accurate for everyone?

BMI is a useful general indicator but has limitations. It doesn't account for muscle mass, bone density, age, or gender. Athletes with high muscle mass may have high BMI scores despite low body fat. Use BMI as one tool among many to assess health.

What types of recipes do you offer?

We offer quick, nutrient-dense recipes for all meals�breakfast bowls, protein-packed lunches, healthy dinners, and smart snacks. Our recipes focus on whole foods, balanced macros, and manageable prep times. All recipes include ingredient lists, instructions, and nutritional information.

Do you have recipes for specific diets?

Yes! Our recipe collection includes options for various dietary approaches including vegetarian, vegan, high-protein, low-carb, and balanced eating. Each recipe is tagged so you can filter by your dietary preferences.

What are diet guides?

Our diet guides provide meal strategy frameworks like the Balanced Plate method, High Protein plans, and Light Evening approaches. These aren't restrictive diets�they're flexible templates you can adapt to your lifestyle and food preferences.

How many calories should I eat per day?

Calorie needs vary based on age, gender, height, weight, and activity level. General guidelines:

  • Sedentary women: 1,600-2,000 calories/day
  • Active women: 2,000-2,400 calories/day
  • Sedentary men: 2,000-2,400 calories/day
  • Active men: 2,400-3,000 calories/day

Tip: Use our nutrition tracker to monitor your intake and see how you feel.

What are macronutrients and why do they matter?

Macronutrients are the three main nutrients your body needs in large amounts:

  • Protein (4 cal/g): Builds and repairs muscle, supports immune function
  • Carbohydrates (4 cal/g): Primary energy source for brain and muscles
  • Fats (9 cal/g): Supports hormone production and nutrient absorption

A balanced ratio might be 40% carbs, 30% protein, 30% fats, but this varies based on your goals.

What's the difference between good and bad carbs?

Complex carbs (good): Whole grains, oats, quinoa, sweet potatoes, legumes. These digest slowly and provide sustained energy.

Simple carbs (limit): White bread, sugary snacks, soda. These spike blood sugar quickly.

How much protein do I really need?

Protein needs depend on your activity level:

  • Sedentary: 0.8g per kg body weight
  • Active: 1.2-1.6g per kg
  • Athletes: 1.6-2.2g per kg

Example: A 70kg active person needs 84-112g protein daily.

Should I avoid fats if I want to lose weight?

No! Healthy fats are essential. Focus on unsaturated fats (avocados, nuts, olive oil, fatty fish). Fats keep you full longer and help with diet adherence. Aim for 20-35% of daily calories from healthy fats.

What's the importance of fiber in my diet?

Fiber is crucial for digestive health, blood sugar control, and satiety. Benefits:

  • Promotes healthy digestion
  • Controls blood sugar levels
  • Lowers cholesterol
  • Keeps you feeling full

Daily target: 25-30g for women, 30-38g for men.

How do I start meal planning as a beginner?

Simple 4-step approach:

  1. Start small: Plan 3-4 dinners for the week
  2. Pick simple recipes from our collection
  3. Make a shopping list
  4. Prep on Sunday: wash vegetables, cook grains

What should I meal prep for the week?

Best foods to prep:

  • Proteins: Grilled chicken, hard-boiled eggs, baked tofu
  • Grains: Brown rice, quinoa (stays good 4-5 days)
  • Vegetables: Roasted broccoli, bell peppers, sweet potatoes

How long can I store meal prepped food?

Storage times:

  • Cooked chicken/meat: 3-4 days
  • Cooked fish: 2-3 days
  • Cooked grains: 4-6 days
  • Cooked vegetables: 4-5 days

What are quick healthy breakfast ideas?

5-minute breakfasts:

  • Overnight oats with berries and nuts
  • Greek yogurt with granola
  • Scrambled eggs with avocado toast
  • Protein smoothie

How do I eat healthy when eating out?

Smart strategies:

  • Check menu online beforehand
  • Ask for dressings on the side
  • Choose grilled over fried
  • Substitute fries for vegetables
  • Take half home for tomorrow

I'm always hungry. What should I do?

Constant hunger usually means you need to adjust:

  • Increase protein: Aim for 25-30g per meal
  • Add fiber: Vegetables and whole grains
  • Don't skip meals: Eat every 3-4 hours
  • Drink water: 8 glasses daily
  • Check calories: You might be eating too little

How do I stop late-night snacking?

Strategies:

  • Eat a larger, protein-rich dinner
  • Brush teeth after dinner
  • Drink herbal tea
  • Keep tempting snacks out
  • Address root cause: boredom or stress

I don't have time to cook. Any tips?

Time-saving hacks:

  • Buy pre-cut vegetables
  • Use a slow cooker
  • Batch cook on weekends
  • Try 5-ingredient recipes
  • Double recipes for leftovers

How do I handle social situations?

Balance is key:

  • Don't arrive starving
  • Fill plate with protein and vegetables first
  • Enjoy special foods guilt-free
  • Return to regular habits next day

What if I miss tracking a day?

Just resume your normal habits the next meal or day. One day doesn't erase weeks of progress. Don't try to "make up" for it by restricting. Consistency over time matters more than perfection.
